Definitions, operations, dot & cross products, projections, and identities

1. What is a vector?

A vector represents a quantity with magnitude and direction. In $\mathbb{R}^n$ we write $\mathbf{v} = (v_1,\dots,v_n)$, and its length (Euclidean norm) is $\lVert \mathbf{v} \rVert = \sqrt{v_1^2+\cdots+v_n^2}$.

2. Notation & components

  • Vectors in bold (e.g., $\mathbf{v}$); scalars in roman (e.g., $a$).
  • 2D: $\mathbf{v}=(v_x, v_y)$; 3D: $\mathbf{v}=(v_x, v_y, v_z)$ with basis $\{\mathbf{i},\mathbf{j},\mathbf{k}\}$.
  • Unit vector: $\hat{\mathbf{v}} = \dfrac{\mathbf{v}}{\lVert \mathbf{v} \rVert}$ (if $\mathbf{v}\neq \mathbf{0}$).

3. Basic operations

Addition: $\ \mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v} = (u_x+v_x,\, u_y+v_y,\, u_z+v_z)$.

Scalar multiplication: $\ a\mathbf{v} = (a v_x,\, a v_y,\, a v_z)$ for $a\in\mathbb{R}$.

Magnitude: $\ \lVert \mathbf{v} \rVert = \sqrt{v_x^2+v_y^2+v_z^2}$.

4. Dot product

Algebraic: $\ \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v} = u_x v_x + u_y v_y + u_z v_z = \sum_{i=1}^n u_i v_i.$ Geometric: $\ \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v} = \lVert \mathbf{u} \rVert \,\lVert \mathbf{v} \rVert \cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$.

Consequences: Cauchyâ€“Schwarz $\ \lvert \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}\rvert \le \lVert \mathbf{u}\rVert \,\lVert \mathbf{v}\rVert$ and orthogonality when $\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=0$.

5. Cross product (3D)

The cross product $\ \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}$ is a vector orthogonal to both, with magnitude $\lVert \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v} \rVert = \lVert \mathbf{u}\rVert \,\lVert \mathbf{v}\rVert \sin\theta$ and direction given by the right-hand rule.

Component form:

$$\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ u_x & u_y & u_z \\ v_x & v_y & v_z \end{vmatrix} = \big(u_y v_z - u_z v_y\big)\mathbf{i} - \big(u_x v_z - u_z v_x\big)\mathbf{j} + \big(u_x v_y - u_y v_x\big)\mathbf{k}.$$

6. Norms, unit vectors & projection

Normalize: $\ \hat{\mathbf{v}} = \dfrac{\mathbf{v}}{\lVert\mathbf{v}\rVert}$. Projection of $\mathbf{v}$ onto $\mathbf{u}\neq\mathbf{0}$: $$\mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}} \mathbf{v} \;=\; \frac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}}\,\mathbf{u} \;=\; \frac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\lVert \mathbf{u}\rVert^2}\,\mathbf{u}.$$

7. Useful identities

  • Distributivity: $\ \mathbf{a}\cdot(\mathbf{b}+\mathbf{c})=\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}+\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{c}$, and similarly for $\times$.
  • Triple product: $\ \mathbf{a}\cdot(\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{c})$ equals the signed volume; cyclic permutations preserve value (up to sign).
  • Vector triple product: $\ \mathbf{a}\times(\mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{c})=\mathbf{b}(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{c})-\mathbf{c}(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b})$.

8. Exercises

  1. Find the angle between $\mathbf{u}=(2,-1,2)$ and $\mathbf{v}=(1,2,2)$.
  2. Compute $\mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{v}$ for $\mathbf{u}=(3,0,4)$ and $\mathbf{v}=(2,1,-1)$.
  3. Show that $\lVert \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}\rVert^2 = \lVert \mathbf{u}\rVert^2 \lVert \mathbf{v}\rVert^2 - (\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v})^2$.
